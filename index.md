# Welcome to H. Eric Tseng's Lab

ETAIC (Emergent Technologies for Autonomy, Intelligence, and Control) Research laboratory focuses on the development of intelligent agents that combine Reinforcement Learning (RL), modern control theory, and game-theoretic human-robot collaboration. Our research explores cutting-edge directions such as Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL), closed-loop integration of Large Language Models (LLMs), and trustworthy autonomy in human-cyber-physical systems.

To bridge theory and practice, ETAIC Lab emphasizes experimental validation. Researchers have full access to a comprehensive suite of robotic platforms and testing facilities, including scaled autonomous vehicles, wheeled robots, robotic arms, and UAVs, supported by a 3D motion capture system and both indoor and outdoor netted drone arenas.

ETAIC Lab is led by Professor Hongtei Eric Tseng, a member of the U.S. National Academy of Engineering (NAE). ETAIC Lab welcomes collaborations across AI, robotics, and control engineering to build the next generation of intelligent, robust, and human-compatible autonomous systems.

## Highlights

{% capture text %}

We explore how cutting-edge AI, control theory, and human-robot interaction can be leveraged to create intelligent agents capable of complex tasks. Our primary research areas encompass the development of intelligent control strategies for micro-mobility robots, including transport and service robots, with a focus on both wheeled and legged platforms like humanoids and quadruped robots. We also delve into multi-robot collaboration and swarm intelligence, enabling teams of robots to seamlessly cooperate for coordinated service delivery. Furthermore, we investigate advanced control techniques for safe and energy-efficient autonomous driving in both autonomous and electric vehicles. To bridge the gap between theoretical advancements and real-world deployment, our lab is equipped with a wide array of robotic platforms, including scaled autonomous vehicles, robotic arms, and unmanned aerial vehicles. These resources, complemented by a 3D motion capture system and specialized drone arenas, allow for rigorous experimental validation of our research findings.

Ongoing projects at the ETAIC Lab push the boundaries of intelligent autonomous systems with practical applications. We are heavily invested in developing adaptive control for humanoid and quadrupedal robots in micro-mobility, focusing on their agility and manipulation in complex environments, validated by our advanced testing facilities. We also explore swarm intelligence for urban mobility, creating collaborative algorithms for drones and autonomous ground vehicles to optimize transportation and delivery in dynamic cityscapes. Furthermore, our research includes trustworthy human-robot collaboration for shared autonomy in driving, developing seamless transitions and enhancing driver understanding in autonomous vehicles. Finally, we are focused on energy-aware predictive control for electric autonomous vehicles, aiming to optimize energy consumption and range through intelligent power management and route planning for sustainable urban mobility.

ETAIC research lab is led by Professor H. Eric Tseng, a distinguished member of the U.S. National Academy of Engineering (NAE) and a recipient of seven Henry Ford Technology Awards. Professor Tseng brings invaluable experience from nearly three decades at Ford Motor Company, where he served as Senior Technical Leader of Controls and Automated Systems. His foundational innovations in vehicle dynamics, advanced powertrain control, and driver-assist technologies have been widely adopted in production vehicles, including the Ford F-150, Lightning, and Super Duty. With over 110 U.S. patents, many in commercial use, and more than 150 coauthored publications, including authoritative book chapters, Professor Tseng provides unparalleled practical insight and academic rigor. This robust leadership, stemming from an NAE member with a proven track record of significant real-world impact, uniquely positions the ETAIC Lab at the forefront of embodied AI and micro-mobility research.
